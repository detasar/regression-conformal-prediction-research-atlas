Regression Conformal Prediction Study

A public evidence atlas for a large empirical study of conformal prediction intervals in regression: experiment scope, result cube, article, supplement, reproducibility notes, and a navigable evidence graph.

Experiment universe

145,839 publication-scoped completed rows across 67 datasets, 95 dataset-alpha cells, and 28 conformal-method labels.

Main empirical signal

Within this retrospective and imbalanced experiment surface, the fixed-GBM CQR pipeline was selected most often under the coverage-gated interval-score rule; Mondrian calibration and CV+ were secondary practical candidates.

CQR backend check

4,564 model-matched CQR runs; 224 paired cells; selected cells fixed-GBM 116, model-matched 71, neither 37. The comparison is reported as backend-sensitivity evidence.

Venn-Abers bridge

The expected strong regression solution did not emerge in these experiments.

Interpretation

These observations are tied to the audited experiment. Deployment use or universal method selection would require separate validation.

Evidence Scope

  • The study reports empirical observations from this audited experiment surface.
  • Production advice, universal method selection, population-level group inference, and bounded-support validity would require separate validation.
  • The Venn-Abers result concerns the evaluated regression bridge and should be read separately from the broader literature.
